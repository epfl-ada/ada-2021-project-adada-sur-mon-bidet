Demographics

We need to know more about our speakers in order to differentiate them on a larger scale, and perhaps spot some peculiar influence on the distribution of their position. Thus, we've chosen to add the gender, age and political affiliation to each speaker. To do so, we've ran a couple hundred of thousands wikepedia searches on the speakers. Some of their pages were neatly completed, and their current political party were filled in as well as their birthdate.

Sadly, it was sometimes, well rarely, ... , never the case. Therefore, we've used some pretty minimalists heuristics to fill in each feature when we couldn't correctly recover the features. For the political affiliation, we simply counted the occcurence of words like "socialist", or "right-wing" in each speakers page. Likewise, for the gender, we count the occurences of gendered pronoums. If we can't find enough of these pronoums, we use a classifer with the name of the speaker as input. Although both of these heuristics are far from perfect, we've found it, most of the time, passed all the sanity checks. Putting all of this together, we get the neat visualisation below.
